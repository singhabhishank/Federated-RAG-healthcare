EXPERIMENT 8: RELEVANCE COMPARISON (FIGURE A - REQUIRED)
================================================================================

Goal: Show that privacy introduces minimal degradation in retrieval quality.

Number of questions per system: 50
Epsilon (for DP systems): 1.0

Relevance Statistics:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
System                              Avg        Median     Std        Degradation    
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Centralized
(No Privacy)            0.967      1.000      0.049      0.00           %
Federated
(No DP)                   0.963      1.000      0.054      0.42           %
Federated + DP
(Privacy)            0.964      1.000      0.054      0.35           %
Federated + DP + SA
(Max Privacy)   0.964      1.000      0.054      0.35           %
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Findings:
- Baseline (Centralized): 0.967 relevance
- Federated (No DP): 0.963 relevance (0.42% degradation)
- Federated + DP: 0.964 relevance (0.35% degradation)
- Federated + DP + SA: 0.964 relevance (0.35% degradation)

Total Privacy Cost: 0.35% relevance loss
Privacy Benefit: Full privacy protection (DP + Secure Aggregation)

Conclusion: Privacy introduces MINIMAL degradation in retrieval quality.
            Even with maximum privacy protection, system maintains 99.7% of baseline performance.
